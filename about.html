About Greg

Childhood

Gregory Scott Camp was born in Bismarck North Dakota in 1957 to Harry and Deloris Camp. He spent his early life in North Dakota He was the youngest of three children having an older brother and sister Steve and Emily. When he was younger he enjoyed playing Baseball. As he got older he would find that music would become the cornerstone in his leasure time.

mid-life

Higher Education

He would leave Bismarck to attend college in South Dakota, first at Mt Marty where he earned his Batchlors, and then USD in Virmillion to earn his Masters. After Graduating from USD, he would move to the Southwest to get his Phd at the University of New Mexico.

Starting a family

While Earning his Masters he and his wife Cindy had their first child, Arlo, who would go on to become a wunderkind chess prodigy. His second child was welcomed into the world two years later. Aaron was clearly his favorite child, while lacking the gifted mind of his other children he saw him as a free spirit pinballing between fortune and dismay like Voltaire's Candide.

Profesional Life

His professional carear began at Minot State University but he would still work to expand his education and authored several publications. He would go on to teach at other Universities, including the University of Tennesee and Lee University. He eventualy moved back to his home town of Bismarck to take a position as a historian for the State Historical Society to be closer to his parents when his father's health began to deteriorate.

Life After Work

Even now in retirement, his days are still focused on his eternal quest for knowledge. He spends his free time reading books on a wide range of topics, from history and political science to astronomy. Though he prefers topical non-fiction works, he will still include a fictional work in His reading list.
